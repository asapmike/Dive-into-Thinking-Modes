Opening

Reasoning is not one thing.

Modern AI systems can answer directly, write intermediate steps, hide private computation, or explore research ideas that reason in hidden state space.

This deck teaches those modes through one small puzzle and one Transformer-based visual language.

Start by telling the audience this is not a ranking of methods. It is a map of where computation becomes visible, hidden, or compressed.
Why it matters

A first DL class already has the right tools.

Students have seen hidden activations, sequence models, attention, and token prediction. Reasoning modes reuse those ideas, but package them in unfamiliar product language.

Visible reasoning

Chain-of-thought makes reasoning a text sequence.

In visible CoT, intermediate steps become tokens. That makes them easy for a human to inspect, and it also gives later generated tokens more context to attend to.

Based on Wei et al. on chain-of-thought prompting, plus scratchpad and zero-shot CoT work.

Mention that visible reasoning is good pedagogically, but not a perfect window into what the model internally did.

Hidden and latent

Hidden reasoning is an interface choice.

The model may use private intermediate computation, but the user sees only an answer or a brief summary. This is closer to many deployed reasoning products.

input private text summary

Latent reasoning is a research direction.

The idea is to spend more computation in hidden activations or special internal steps before producing text. It is promising, but should not be presented as a standard deployed mechanism.

input hidden state loop answer
This slide is where we avoid overclaiming. Hidden reasoning and latent reasoning are related, but not the same claim.
Tradeoffs

The choice is a design tradeoff, not a simple upgrade.

Mode Visible tokens Transparency Token cost Research maturity
Direct answer Low Low Low Standard baseline
Visible CoT High Higher Higher Well-studied prompting method
Hidden reasoning Medium Medium Varies Common product pattern
Latent preview Low Lower Potentially lower output cost Emerging research
Stress that transparency, efficiency, and faithfulness do not all improve at once. The artifact helps students reason about the tradeoff.
